# Impact Bullets

## Purpose

Turn responsibility descriptions into evidence-backed bullets that show scope, action, and result.

## What Good Looks Like

A strong bullet answers: what changed, who benefited, how big was it, and what did the candidate actually do?

## Weak vs Strong

Weak: Responsible for backend APIs.

Strong: Built and maintained payment APIs used by 40k monthly customers, reducing checkout failures by 18% through idempotency fixes and alerting improvements.

Weak: Worked on dashboards.

Strong: Launched self-serve analytics dashboards for sales leadership, cutting weekly manual reporting work from 6 hours to 45 minutes.

## Role-Specific Notes

- Engineering: latency, reliability, cost, defects, scale, developer productivity.
- Product: activation, retention, revenue, adoption, experiment result, customer segment.
- Data/AI: model metric, decision quality, pipeline reliability, analysis adoption.
- Management: team size, delivery predictability, hiring, retention, incident reduction.

## Common Mistakes

- Starting every bullet with "Responsible for".
- Adding inflated numbers the candidate cannot explain.
- Hiding the actual action behind team-level outcomes.
- Using internal project names without context.
