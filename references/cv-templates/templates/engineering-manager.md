# Engineering Manager CV Template

## Target Roles

Engineering manager, software engineering manager, platform engineering manager, data engineering manager, senior engineering manager, head of engineering.

## When to Use

Use when the JD emphasizes team leadership, delivery, hiring, coaching, technical strategy, cross-functional execution, reliability, or scaling engineering organizations.

## Section Order

1. Header
2. Leadership summary
3. Leadership/technical skills
4. Management experience
5. Earlier technical experience
6. Education/certifications

## Reconstructed Skeleton

### Summary

Engineering leader managing <team size/scope> across <product/platform/domain>. Experienced in <delivery/system area>, hiring/coaching, and partnering with product/design/business to deliver <business or technical outcome>.

### Skills

- Leadership: hiring, coaching, performance management, career growth, team health
- Delivery: roadmap planning, execution, Agile/Scrum/Kanban, incident management
- Technical: architecture reviews, cloud/platform, reliability, data/security where relevant
- Business: stakeholder alignment, prioritization, budgeting/vendor management if truthful

### Experience Bullet Pattern

- Managed <team size> engineers delivering <product/platform>, improving <delivery/reliability/business metric> by <number>.
- Hired/coached <scope>, resulting in <promotion/retention/productivity/team-health outcome>.
- Led <cross-functional initiative> across <teams>, reducing <risk/cost/time> and improving <customer/business outcome>.

## Bullet Style

Balance people leadership, technical credibility, and business results. Quantify team scope, delivery outcomes, reliability gains, hiring velocity, retention, or stakeholder impact.

## Optimization Goal

Show that the candidate improves teams and systems, not just attends planning meetings.

## Pitfalls

- Writing like an IC with no team outcomes.
- Writing like a project manager with no technical judgment.
- Claiming team results without clarifying role and scope.
- Omitting people-development evidence.
