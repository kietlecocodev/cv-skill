# Keyword Matching

## Actionable Rule

Extract must-have requirements from the JD, then mirror exact terminology only when the candidate has real evidence for that skill or responsibility.

## Why It Matters

ATS databases and recruiters often search for role-specific terms. Exact wording helps only when paired with credible context.

## Safe Examples

- JD says “PostgreSQL”; CV says “PostgreSQL” in Skills and shows a production database bullet.
- JD says “roadmap prioritization”; PM bullet describes a real roadmap decision and outcome.

## Unsafe Examples

- Adding every JD keyword to Skills with no supporting work.
- Using hidden text or repeated keyword blocks.
- Replacing truthful adjacent experience with inflated exact-match claims.
