# ATS Formatting

## Actionable Rule

Use a clean, single-column structure with standard section headings, readable text, and minimal decorative layout unless the target company explicitly asks for a portfolio-style CV.

## Why It Matters

Many applicant tracking systems and recruiter workflows parse resumes into structured fields. Complex layouts can make parsing less reliable and slow down human scanning.

## Safe Examples

- Standard headings: Summary, Skills, Experience, Projects, Education.
- Reverse chronological experience.
- Text-based bullets.
- Clear dates and company names.
- Links written as readable URLs or standard hyperlinks.

## Unsafe Examples

- Heavy tables for core content.
- Multi-column layouts that split dates and descriptions unpredictably.
- Icons replacing text labels.
- Keyword stuffing in hidden text.
- Graphics-only skill bars.
