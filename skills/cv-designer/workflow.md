# CV Designer Workflow

## Purpose

Run an end-to-end CV tailoring process from candidate input, JD, and company context.

## Workflow

1. **Intake** — collect candidate experience/CV, target JD, company website/context, target market, seniority, constraints.
2. **Candidate Profile Extraction** — extract roles, achievements, metrics, skills, projects, education, constraints, sensitive claims, and uncertain claims.
3. **JD Analysis** — identify must-haves, nice-to-haves, responsibilities, keywords, seniority signals, and hidden priorities.
4. **Company Analysis** — infer company stage, product/business model, culture/tone, likely hiring signals, and CV tone implications.
5. **Match Strategy** — map strongest overlaps, gaps, transferable evidence, and claims to avoid.
6. **CV Strategy Options** — propose 2-4 CV directions before drafting.
7. **User Direction Gate** — wait for user selection or approval.
8. **Draft CV** — write selected version using real evidence, target keywords, and reference library patterns.
9. **Review** — run truthfulness, ATS, recruiter scan, keyword, and readability checks.
10. **Final Output** — provide final CV plus change notes, missing info, and interview risk notes.

## Hard Gate

Do not draft final CV until the user chooses or approves a CV strategy option.

## Reference Use

- Use `references/cv-templates/` for role-family templates and section patterns.
- Use `references/ats-keywords/` for keyword maps and ATS/recruiter best practices.
- References guide structure and wording; they do not override candidate truth.
